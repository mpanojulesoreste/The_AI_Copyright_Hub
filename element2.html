Navigating the Ethical and Social Implications of AI and Copyrights

With the complicated relationship between copyright protections and the ever evolving world of artificial intelligence, there come many social and ethical implications that need to be considered when exploring the dynamic duo.

Access to Knowledge: Various fair dealing laws allow for the knowledge from certain intellectual properties to be evenly dispersed to communities, both “affluent” and underserved. The Fair Use Doctrine allows intellectual property to be protected while allowing large audiences to access and use the product to certain extents, that way the technology is not exclusive due to copyright law, and is instead able to be accessed and disperse knowledge and creativity to the masses.
 

Basic Constitutional Freedoms: There rises the ethical question of freedom of speech and expression with abiding my copyright laws. With the laws, people are restricted from saying or doing certain things they might consider under the realm of their given freedoms under the Constitution. For example, Parody and Satire are protected under the Constitution, but there are certain conflicts between what is considered “parody” versus the replication of copyrighted material.

Censorship: There is an issue of potential censorship when Takedown Notices are given as copyright enforcement. This can limit acts of dissent, criticism, and other negative views on a thing or topic, which fall under free speech and expression rights.
 

Ethical/Social Implications of AI

Privacy: A large and common ethical dilemma with AI is privacy and surveillance of an individual. As AI systems operate, they collect and analyze data to produce a specific and tailored answer to the user based on their given question and/or data. This can potentially lead to the collection of personal information and tendencies that can in turn create privacy and surveillance controversy.

Employment Opportunities: A social implication of AI can be seen in the workforce. As AI develops, machines are able to do more activities at a much faster and accurate rate than humans. This has led to an increase in machines doing jobs that humans were previously employed to do. This creates a shortage of jobs and has impacted unemployment greatly in recent years.

Transparency: The lack of transparency regarding how AI systems operate and reach their decisions can lead to an ethical concern of security and bias. We as users can’t know if the system is giving an unbiased, computed answer, or if it has been tampered with to produce a biased output.
